Climathon 2016

2016-01-07, Trondheim, Norway

The problem

At the Climathon event three problems were addressed:

  • How can we use existing open data sets to calibrate and check official reported emissions to better understand city emissions?
  • How can the data from ground-level emissions sensors be integrated and used to drive change?
  • Existing sensor systems are expensive. How can we build cheaper, open source sensor systems?

The situation today

For many years, Trondheim Municipality depended on yearly reports from Statistics Norway (SSB).

Because of uncertainties regarding the quality of this data, SSB decided to discontinue these yearly reports in 2012.1

Trondheim Municipality is therefore acting somewhat in the dark when trying to figure out how to reach the goal of reducing the municipality's GHG emission levels by 25% by 2020 and 70-90% by 2050 (compared to 1991 levels).2

Our approach

Real time data, not annual reports

During the 24 hour hackathon we made a sensor that measured the CO2 level and reported this in real time to the cloud. We then retrieved this data through an open API and displayed it on a website. This way, the municipality can get immediate feedback on the effects of policies.
